1. Why this is mandatory now

The live privacy policy and the Play Data Safety form both say Niyam collects no personal data and has no accounts / no servers. The backend phase made that false: we now require Google Sign-In and store practice + entitlement data in Supabase. Shipping the next signed AAB with a "we collect nothing" policy + Data Safety form would be a false declaration — a common Play rejection/suspension cause and a DPDP-Act problem. This phase makes both documents truthful before the next release.

In plain English: we told users and Google "we keep nothing about you." That stopped being true the moment we added sign-in and sync. We have to fix the wording before we upload the next build, or Google can pull the app.

3. Privacy policy — proposed new wording

Changes to website/privacy.html (effective date bumped; meta description updated). Old → new for each affected section:

"The short version"

Now: "Niyam has no accounts and no sign-up. We do not collect, store, or transmit your personal data. Your mantras, journeys, streaks and settings live only on your device."
New:
Niyam asks you to sign in with Google so your practice can sync across your devices. We store your email and your practice — your mantras, journeys, intention and favourites — on our secure servers (Supabase), tied to your account. We never see your payment details, and we never read or collect your activity in any other app. You can delete your account and everything we hold, any time, from inside the app.

New section — "Your account & synced practice"

Add:
When you sign in with Google, we receive your email address and a Google account identifier. We use them only to create your Niyam account and to attach your synced practice to it. We store your current mantra, sadhana progress, completed mantras, chosen intention, display language and favourites so they follow you to a new phone. This data is hosted on our behalf by Supabase and protected so that only your signed-in account can read it. We do not sell it, share it for advertising, or use it to track you across other apps or services.

"What stays on your device" (trim to what's genuinely local)

New list: blocked-app list · theme preference · reading-history/streak counters · the accessibility detection itself. (Mantra/progress/intention/language/favourites move to the synced section above.)

Replace the false "no servers" line

Now: "Uninstalling the app deletes all of it. We have no servers and keep no copy."
New:
Uninstalling removes everything stored on your device. Your account and synced practice remain on our servers until you delete them — use Settings → Delete account in the app to erase your account and all synced data immediately, or write to our grievance contact below.

Data deletion & retention (new short section)

Add:
Deleting your account from the app permanently removes your profile, synced practice, favourites and entitlement records from our servers; this cannot be undone. We keep your synced data only while your account exists.

Children & DPDP — correct the "collects no personal data" claim

Now: DPDP section says "Because the app collects no personal data, most processing simply never occurs."
New: we now hold your email + synced practice, so we state the DPDP rights as actually exercisable — access, correction, erasure (the in-app Delete account), grievance to our contact (7-day ack / 90-day resolve), and recourse to the Data Protection Board. Children section keeps the 18+ intent but drops the "no personal data is processed" sentence.

Sub-processors (new one-liner)

Add: "We rely on Supabase (account, database, hosting) and Google (sign-in, Play billing, AdMob) to operate Niyam. Each processes data only to provide its service."

Unchanged: Accessibility Service section (still on-device-only, accurate), Purchases (Google Play), Advertising (AdMob free tier), Google Play AccessibilityService disclosure, website waitlist.
