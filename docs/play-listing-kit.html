Full description (4000 chars max)

Most app blockers are walls — timers, locks, and shame. People uninstall them within a week, because nobody wants to live with a jailer.

Niyam is a threshold.

When you open an app you chose to block — Instagram, YouTube, Facebook — Niyam appears first with a mantra you selected: the Gayatri, the Mahamrityunjaya, Om Namah Shivaya, a verse of the Bhagavad Gita. You read it, in your own script, with its meaning, for 15 seconds while a gentle ring fills. Then you continue, and the app opens normally. The scroll is never forbidden. It is consecrated.

THE PRACTICE
• Each read counts toward a 14-day sadhana — one mantra, practiced daily
• Streaks build gently; finishing a journey is celebrated, then you choose the next
• After Continue, Niyam stays out of your way for five minutes — no nagging

THE LIBRARY
• 26 authentic mantras with source citations: Vedic (Gayatri — Rig Veda 3.62.10, Mahamrityunjaya), Upanishadic shanti mantras, Bhagavad Gita verses (2.47, 2.70, 18.66…), and devotional classics (Hanuman Chalisa opening, Om Namah Shivaya)
• 9 languages, completely: Hindi, Sanskrit, Marathi, Telugu, Tamil, Kannada, Bengali, Gujarati, English — the mantra in its native script, its meaning, and the entire app interface

PRIVACY AS PRINCIPLE
• No account. No sign-up. No personal data collected — ever
• Everything stays on your device; we have no servers
• The accessibility service detects only the launch of apps you chose to block — it cannot and does not read your screen, messages, or anything else

FAIR AND FREE
• Blocking and the mantra pause are free forever (5 starter mantras, English + Hindi, small banner ads on the home and library screens only)
• Premium — ₹15/week, ₹49/month, or ₹399/year with a 7-day full trial — unlocks all 26 mantras, all 9 languages, unlimited switching, and removes ads
• Three vows: the mantra screen never carries an ad, is never locked behind payment, and an active journey is never broken by a paywall

4e. AccessibilityService declaration (the one that matters most)

Console will ask why the app uses the AccessibilityService API and whether it's an accessibility tool.

IsAccessibilityTool: No.

Declaration text:
Niyam is a digital-wellbeing app. It uses the AccessibilityService API
(TYPE_WINDOW_STATE_CHANGED events only) for exactly one core function: detecting
the moment a user-selected app (e.g. Instagram, YouTube, Facebook) is opened, so
that the user's chosen mantra can be displayed for a 15-second mindful pause
before that app is used. This is the app's single core feature and cannot be
implemented with reasonable alternatives at equivalent reliability
(UsageStatsManager polling is battery-intensive and too slow for an
at-launch-moment overlay).

The service does not read, capture, store, or transmit screen content, text,
or personal data. Window-state events are processed entirely on-device and
discarded. Before the service can be enabled, the app shows a prominent
in-app disclosure describing this exact use and requires the user's explicit
affirmative consent ("I agree — turn on"); consent is recorded on-device.
The user can disable the service at any time in system Accessibility settings.

Review sometimes requests a short screen-recording showing: disclosure screen → consent tap → system settings → service on → blocked app opens → mantra overlay appears. I can produce this from the emulator the moment they ask.
