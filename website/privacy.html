Privacy Policy

Effective 16 June 2026 · Applies to the Niyam Android app and www.myniyam.com

The short version

Niyam asks you to sign in with Google so your practice can sync across your devices. We store your email and your practice — your mantras, journeys, intention and favourites — on our secure servers (Supabase), tied to your account. We never see your payment details, and we never read or collect your activity in any other app. You can delete your account and everything we hold, any time, from inside the app.

Your account & synced practice

When you sign in with Google, we receive your email address and a Google account identifier. We use them only to create your Niyam account and to attach your synced practice to it. We store your current mantra, sadhana progress, completed mantras, chosen intention, display language and favourites so they follow you to a new phone. This data is hosted on our behalf by Supabase and protected so that only your signed-in account can read it. We do not sell it, share it for advertising, or use it to track you across other apps or services.

Accessibility Service

Niyam uses Android's Accessibility Service for exactly one purpose: to detect the moment you open an app you chose to block (such as Instagram, Facebook or YouTube), so your mantra can appear first. It does not read, record, or transmit any content from your screen, your messages, or any other app. Detection happens entirely on your device.

What stays on your device

  • Your blocked-app list and the accessibility detection itself
  • Your theme preference
  • Your reading-history and streak counters

Uninstalling removes everything stored on your device. Your account and synced practice remain on our servers until you delete them — use Settings → Delete account in the app to erase your account and all synced data immediately, or write to our grievance contact below.

Deleting your data

Deleting your account from the app permanently removes your profile, synced practice, favourites and entitlement records from our servers. This cannot be undone. We keep your synced data only while your account exists.

Purchases

Premium subscriptions are processed by Google Play. We never see or store your payment details. Google's handling of your payment information is governed by Google's Privacy Policy.

Advertising

The free tier shows banner ads served by Google AdMob on the Home and Library screens only — never on the mantra screen. AdMob may process device identifiers to serve ads; see Google's AdMob privacy information. Premium and trial users see no ads.

Who we rely on

We rely on Supabase (your account, database and hosting) and Google (sign-in, Play billing and AdMob) to operate Niyam. Each processes data only to provide its service.

This website

If you join the waitlist, your email address is delivered to us by a form service and used only to tell you when Niyam launches. No analytics or tracking cookies run on this site.

Children

Niyam is a general-audience devotional app intended for users aged 18 or over. We do not knowingly collect data from children. If you believe a child has created an account, write to our grievance contact below and we will delete it.

Your rights under Indian law (DPDP Act, 2023)

We operate under India's Digital Personal Data Protection Act, 2023 and its Rules. For the personal data we hold — your account email and synced practice, plus any waitlist or grievance email — you have the right to access it, correct it, have it erased, and to complain. You can erase your account and all synced data yourself at any time via Settings → Delete account in the app. You may also write to our grievance contact at netipranavaditya@gmail.com; we aim to acknowledge within 7 days and resolve within 90 days. If unsatisfied, you may approach India's Data Protection Board. In the unlikely event of a data breach affecting you, we will notify you and the Board as the Rules require.

Google Play disclosures

In line with Google Play's policy on the AccessibilityService API, the app presents a prominent in-app disclosure and asks for your explicit consent before the service is enabled. The service is used solely to detect launches of apps you chose to block; it does not collect, store, or transmit screen content or personal data.

Changes & contact

If this policy changes, the new version will appear on this page with a new effective date. Questions and grievances: netipranavaditya@gmail.com. See also our Terms & Conditions.
